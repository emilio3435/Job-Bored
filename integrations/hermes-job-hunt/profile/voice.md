# Emilio Nunez-Garcia — Voice Guide

Use this guide to write resumes, cover letters, LinkedIn notes, recruiter replies, application answers, and interview prep in Emilio’s voice.

## Voice summary

Direct, precise, scannable. Operator’s voice: short clauses, specific nouns, almost no filler. Sells with proof, not adjectives. Confident without performing confidence. Quietly unit-economics fluent: CAC, LTV, ROAS, payback, contribution margin, and marginal return are normal vocabulary. Warmly irreverent on personal-brand surfaces; clean and executive-ready in resumes and formal applications.

Signature thesis: “Ship paid spend toward marginal ROAS.”

Primary positioning: digital marketing consultant + AI product builder. Sales leadership is evidence, not the headline.

## What the voice should sound like

- Specific. “Western Dental, 200+ DSO locations” beats “large healthcare client.” “tROAS portfolio bid strategy” beats “advanced bidding.”
- Concrete verbs. Built, shipped, ran, carried, owned, deployed, reallocated, architected, routed, optimized, forecasted, coached.
- Short clauses. Sentence lands. Next sentence lands.
- Numbers where verified. $10M+ book. Top-3 national. #19 market. 130% YoY paid-search conversion growth. 13% YoY new-user lift. ~60% digital mix. 21+ forecasts against $2.4M pipeline. 20+ pitches/month. 10 AE desks. 200+ Western Dental locations. 500+ Kawasaki dealers. Three DCMs. Eight years. Four roles.
- Strategic, then grounded. One zoom-out sentence, then a concrete proof point.
- Human but not fluffy. Warmth is allowed in cover letters and LinkedIn messages; resumes stay spare.
- AI grounded in use cases. Forecasting, briefings, reporting workflows, RAG, routing, job-board scans, resume tailoring — not “AI visionary.”

## What the voice should not sound like

- Generic SaaS marketer.
- AI hype.
- Resume cliché stack.
- Pure engineer, unless the role specifically requires technical-adjacent framing.
- Overly humble or apologetic about the nontraditional path.
- Company flattery as an opener.
- Sales-first positioning.
- Compensation discussion in outreach materials.
- Pejorative departure framing.

## Resume voice rules

For page-fit, evidence coverage, and QA checks, read `materials-quality.md` before drafting application materials.

1. Lead with “performance marketing leader and the systems behind it” or “digital marketing consultant and AI product builder,” depending on role.
2. Start bullets with strong verbs.
3. One bullet = one accomplishment.
4. Use verified metrics only.
5. Prefer specific channels, clients, and systems over broad claims.
6. Use approved client names freely where relevant: Western Dental, Premier Dentist Partners, Kids Choice Dental, Credit Union of Denver, Bellco Credit Union, Westerra Credit Union, Brothers Plumbing Heating & Electric, Steele Brothers Heating & Air, Mr. Rooter Plumbing, Texas Ace Heating & Air, Eden Air HVAC, Carrier dealer networks, national Kawasaki co-op, PXG, Kalahari Resorts, UNC.

8. Certifications are historical unless Emilio confirms current validity.
9. Use public Audacy date framing: Sep 2017 → April 2026.
10. Keep the resume clean. No emojis, skill bars, star ratings, photos, badges, or gimmicks.

## Cover letter voice rules

Target shape: three short paragraphs.

1. Hook: most relevant overlap between Emilio and the role.
2. Evidence: one compact Audacy proof point and one compact AI-builder proof point.
3. Close: what Emilio would help the company do; direct ask.

Rules:

- No flattery opener.
- No compensation expectations.
- Hyperlink projects when supported.
- One small line of personality is allowed; dial irreverence to about 30% of portfolio voice.
- Do not apologize for the nontraditional path. Frame it as the advantage.
- Departure framing, if needed: part of a broader Audacy restructure / layoff. Do not dwell.

## LinkedIn / recruiter message voice rules

- Maximum 4–5 sentences.
- Open with a sentence that earns the next one.
- Include one specific proof point: a metric, a project, or a role-specific reason.
- Ask for a low-friction next step: 15-minute call, fit check, permission to send tailored resume.
- Sound warm and senior, not needy.
- Avoid “Hope this finds you well” and “I came across your profile and was impressed.”
- No compensation discussion unless the recruiter explicitly asks.

## Interview answer voice rules

- Answer first, story second.
- Use compressed STAR: situation, action, result, lesson.
- Name tradeoffs.
- For AI answers: name the model/tool, use case, user/workflow, constraint, and outcome.
- For performance answers: name the bid strategy, audience layer, measurement framework, and business metric.
- If a detail is uncertain, say: “I’d want to verify the exact number, but the shape was...” Do not guess.
