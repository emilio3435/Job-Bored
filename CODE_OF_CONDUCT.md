# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

JobBored is a small project that anyone can fork, run, and shape. To keep the
community welcoming for newcomers, experienced contributors, and people who
just want to ship better job applications, everyone interacting with this
repo — issues, pull requests, discussions, code review — agrees to behave with
basic decency.

## What this looks like in practice

**Do:**

- Be kind, even when you're right. Especially when you're right.
- Critique the code, the decision, the idea. Not the person.
- Assume good faith. Most "weird code" was written by someone solving a
  problem you can't see from the diff.
- When you correct someone, lead with what they got right.
- When you're corrected, accept it gracefully if it's fair, push back
  thoughtfully if it isn't.

**Don't:**

- Personal attacks, insults, or sustained sarcasm at a contributor's expense.
- Harassment, including unwanted DMs, doxxing, sexual attention, or targeted
  pile-ons.
- Discrimination based on race, gender, sexuality, disability, religion,
  nationality, body, age, or experience level.
- Gatekeeping ("everyone knows this", "RTFM"). New contributors are the
  point.

## What happens if someone crosses the line

The maintainer (currently [@emilio3435](https://github.com/emilio3435)) will:

1. **Talk to them privately** first. Most problems are solved here.
2. **Edit or remove** the offending content if it stays public after that.
3. **Temporary or permanent ban** from this repo's issues, PRs, and
   discussions if the behavior continues or is severe.

There is no appeals committee. The maintainer's call is final for this repo.

## How to report

For anything you'd rather not say in public, email
**emilio@elioai.app** with a link to the conversation and a one-line
description. You'll get an acknowledgement within 72 hours.

## Scope

This applies wherever the project shows up: issues, PRs, discussions, the
repo's social spaces (if any are added later), and one-on-one communication
that started in this project's spaces.
